Guide

Here is the short information about the Abbreviations, Units and Parameters on this page

Abbreviations

  • AVS: Acoustic Vector Sensor
  • DoA: Direction of Arrival
  • RNL: Received Noise Level
  • SNR: Signal-to-Noise Ratio

Units

  • Meters (m):
    Unit of distance for Range.
  • Decibels (dB):
    Unit of sound pressure level for RNL and SNR.
  • Degrees (deg):
    Unit of angular measurement for DoA, Estimated DoA, and DoA Error.

Parameters

  • Range:
    The distance between the AVS sensor and the detected object.
  • Received Noise Level (RNL):
    The strength of the background noise received by the AVS sensor. Measured in decibels (dB).
  • Signal-to-Noise Ratio (SNR):
    The ratio of the strength of the desired signal (from the target object) to the background noise level. Measured in decibels (dB). Higher SNR indicates a clearer signal.
  • Actual DoA:
    The true direction of arrival of the sound wave from the target object, relative to the AVS sensor. Measured in degrees (deg).
  • Estimated DoA:
    The direction of arrival of the sound wave as estimated by the AVS system, based on its processing of the received signal. Measured in degrees (deg).
  • DoA Error:
    The difference between the actual DoA and the estimated DoA. Measured in degrees (deg). A lower DoA error indicates a more accurate estimation.
